PHILIPPA

[ visit the neural network ]

I attempted to teach moral values to a neural network I call Philippa by training her on a dataset containing people's responses to five kinds of moral dilemmas.

Imagine you're a driver of a trolley car that is hurtling down the track at sixty miles per hour. You notice that there are five workers working towards the end of the track. The brakes are faulty, and you feel desperate because you know that if the trolley crashes into these people, they will surely die.

But then you notice a sidetrack off to the right. There is one worker working on that track. Your steering wheel works. You can steer the trolley car, if you want to, onto this sidetrack—killing the one worker, but sparing five.

Which is the more ethical option? To do nothing and kill the five workers working on the track, or to steer the trolley car onto the sidetrack and kill the one singular worker?

Now, imagine you’re a transplant surgeon. Five patients have come to your clinic, each in dire need of an organ. One needs a lung, two a kidney, one a heart, and one a liver. There are no donors, and these patients are going to die. All of a sudden, a healthy patient walks into your clinic for a regular checkup and he’s seated in another room. You could, if you wanted to, cut up the healthy patient, yank out his organs and save the other five patients, effectively killing the one healthy patient.

Despite the two situations bearing the same conflict between their consequences, our moral intuition will tell us that they are all inherently different from one another. That is because there are more moral variables at play than just the number of lives.

A machine understands arithmatic comparisions. 5 will always be greater than 1, yet Philippa works on a framework that accounts for other moral variables like: desire, intent, and duty. It was trained on people's responses to five thought experiments, all of which weigh 5 lives against 1. The responses were collected from over 350 people at several popular spots in New Delhi like Hauz Khas, Cannaught Place, and the Khan Market.
