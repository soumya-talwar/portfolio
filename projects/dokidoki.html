DOKI-DOKI

[ play the game ]

Doki-doki is an otome game where men get a chance to win a date with me.

Since there are many men who haven't had the opportunity to date me, I thought I'd offer them a virtual experience of what it's like to go out with me.

In an industry that predominantly caters to its male audience, otome games stand out as a popular exception in Japan that are marketed to young women. Otome games are story-based dating simulators that primarily use dialogue-based progression and typically feature a female protagonist who can develop a romantic relationship with one of the male supporting characters.

An interesting aside is that "Soumya" is actually my fourth name. My third name was supposed to be "Simar" which means "to pray" in Hindi, but that was a time of no internet and my parents reached out to the closest English-speaking relative to confirm how my name should be spelt in English. This individual opened the Oxford Dictionary and gave my parents the spelling for "Simmer" (i.e. "boiling" in English). Added to that was my surname "Talwar" which means "sword" in Hindi, and it didn't help that I had a bad temper to compliment my name. My grandparents blamed my name, and my name was changed to "Soumya" which means "calm" in Hindi. I used to be a hot sword, and now I am a calm sword.

Why this is relevant is because all dateable characters in the game have been given my names (Smriti, Simmer, and Soumya; Simran was another name, but due to its very short duration, it doesn't appear in the game). The personalities of these characters mirror the personalities I had when I held those names.

The player progresses in the game by conversing with me like we would be on a date, and if they give the right answers, their contact details will be shared with me over mail and I can ask them out to a real date.
